# Multi-Jurisdiction Compliance Report  
**Company:** ShopGhana  
**Subject:** Data Deletion Requests – Ghana, European Union, and California  
**Author:** Nana Kwaku Sarpong


## 1. Introduction

This report analyzes three data deletion requests received by ShopGhana from customers located in Ghana, Germany (European Union), and California (United States). Each request is governed by a distinct data protection framework:

- Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) – Ghana  
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) – European Union  
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) – California, USA  

The purpose of this report is to evaluate the legal rights of each customer, determine ShopGhana’s compliance obligations, outline required action steps, and identify applicable response deadlines and exemptions.


# 2. Customer A – Abena (Ghana)

## 2.1 Applicable Legal Framework  
Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)

## 2.2 Legal Right to Deletion

Under Act 843, Abena has a **conditional right** to request the erasure or destruction of her personal data. Deletion may be required where:

- The purpose for collection no longer exists;  
- Processing is unlawful; or  
- Retention is no longer necessary.  

The Act does not provide an absolute “right to be forgotten” comparable to the GDPR.

## 2.3 ShopGhana’s Obligations

ShopGhana must:

- Respond within **21 days** of receiving the request;  
- Assess whether continued retention is legally required;  
- Delete non-essential personal data;  
- Retain data required for statutory tax and accounting compliance;  
- Inform Abena of the outcome and explain any retained data.

## 2.4 Data Retention Considerations

**May be retained:**

- Transaction and invoicing records (tax compliance);  
- Accounting documentation;  
- Fraud prevention and security logs.  

**Typical retention period:** Up to 6 years (financial record-keeping requirements).

**Must be deleted:**

- Marketing data;  
- Behavioral analytics information;  
- Account login credentials;  
- Non-essential profile data.


## 2.5 Draft Response – Abena (Ghana)

**Subject: Response to Data Deletion Request**

Dear Abena,

We acknowledge receipt of your request to delete your account and personal information under the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843).

Following review, your account profile, marketing preferences, and other non-essential personal data have been deleted from our active systems.

Please note that certain transaction and accounting records must be retained to comply with statutory tax and financial record-keeping obligations under Ghanaian law. These records will be securely stored, restricted from non-essential processing, and deleted after the legally required retention period expires.

If you require further clarification, please contact our Data Protection Officer.

Kind regards,  
**ShopGhana**


# 3. Customer B – Lukas (Germany – GDPR)

## 3.1 Applicable Legal Framework  
General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), Article 17

## 3.2 Legal Right to Erasure

Lukas has a strong and explicit right to erasure under **Article 17 GDPR**. The right applies where:

- Data is no longer necessary;  
- Consent is withdrawn;  
- Processing is unlawful;  
- The individual objects to processing.

## 3.3 Exemptions

Article 17(3) permits retention where processing is necessary for:

- Compliance with a legal obligation;  
- Tax and accounting requirements;  
- Establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.

## 3.4 Required Action Steps

ShopGhana must:

1. Verify Lukas’s identity;  
2. Log and document the request;  
3. Cease non-essential processing;  
4. Delete account and marketing data;  
5. Restrict retained financial records;  
6. Notify third-party processors;  
7. Confirm completion in writing.

## 3.5 Response Deadline

- **1 month** from receipt of request;  
- Extension of up to 2 additional months if necessary (with notice).

## 3.6 Penalties for Non-Compliance

Failure to comply may result in:

- Regulatory investigation;  
- Administrative fines up to **€20 million or 4% of global annual turnover**, whichever is higher.


## 3.7 Draft Response – Lukas (Germany – GDPR)

**Subject: Confirmation of Erasure Request – Article 17 GDPR**

Dear Lukas,

We confirm receipt of your request for erasure pursuant to Article 17 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Your account profile, marketing data, and associated identifiers have been erased from our systems. We have also notified relevant third-party service providers to delete your personal data where applicable.

Certain transaction records are retained solely to comply with applicable tax and accounting obligations under European Union law. These records are restricted from further processing and will be retained only for the legally mandated period.

If you require further information or wish to exercise additional rights under the GDPR, you may contact our Data Protection Officer or your local supervisory authority.

Kind regards,  
**ShopGhana**


# 4. Customer C – Maria (California – CCPA/CPRA)

## 4.1 Applicable Legal Framework  
California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)  
California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA)

## 4.2 Rights Available

Maria has the right to:

- Request deletion of personal information;  
- Know what personal information is collected and shared;  
- Opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information;  
- Non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights.

## 4.3 Deletion Request Analysis

Because Maria has an active return dispute, ShopGhana may retain personal information necessary to:

- Complete the transaction;  
- Detect security incidents;  
- Exercise or defend legal claims.

Deletion may therefore be delayed until the dispute is resolved.

## 4.4 “Do Not Sell or Share” Request

ShopGhana must:

- Immediately process the opt-out request;  
- Add Maria to a “Do Not Sell/Share” suppression list;  
- Cease selling or sharing her personal information;  
- Notify relevant third parties where required.

## 4.5 Response Deadline

- **45 days** from receipt;  
- May extend once by an additional 45 days with notice.

## 4.6 Required Disclosures

ShopGhana must disclose:

- Categories of personal information collected;  
- Categories sold or shared;  
- Purpose of processing;  
- Categories of third parties;  
- Explanation for any partial denial of deletion.


4.7 Draft Response – Maria (California – CCPA/CPRA)

**Subject: Response to CCPA/CPRA Privacy Request**

Dear Maria,

We acknowledge receipt of your request under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA).

Your request to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information has been processed effective immediately. Your information will no longer be sold or shared with third parties for commercial purposes.

With respect to your deletion request, we are currently required to retain certain personal information due to an active return dispute associated with your recent transaction. This retention is necessary to complete the transaction and to protect our legal rights. Upon resolution of the dispute, we will proceed with deletion in accordance with applicable law.

You may request additional details regarding the categories of personal information we collect, use, or disclose at any time.

Kind regards,  
**ShopGhana**


# 5. Comparative Analysis

| Element | Ghana DPA | GDPR | CCPA/CPRA |
|----------|------------|--------|------------|
| Right to Deletion | Conditional | Strong and explicit | Yes |
| Exemptions | Legal obligations, public interest | Legal obligation, legal claims | Active transaction, legal compliance |
| Response Deadline | 21 days | 1 month | 45 days |
| Penalties | Administrative fines; possible criminal liability | Up to €20M or 4% turnover | Up to $7,500 per violation |
| Consent Model | Lawful basis required | Lawful basis + strict consent rules | Opt-out for sale/share |


# 6. Practical Implementation Challenges

ShopGhana must manage:

- Different statutory response deadlines;  
- Varying scope of deletion rights;  
- Cross-border tax retention requirements;  
- Third-party processor coordination;  
- Multi-jurisdiction regulatory exposure;  
- Active dispute handling alongside deletion rights.


# 7. Conclusion

ShopGhana must implement a jurisdiction-sensitive compliance workflow capable of:

- Tracking jurisdiction-specific deadlines;  
- Applying lawful exemptions appropriately;  
- Restricting rather than fully deleting data where legally required;  
- Documenting compliance decisions;  
- Communicating transparently with customers.

Failure to properly distinguish between Ghana’s Data Protection Act, the GDPR, and the CCPA/CPRA exposes the company to significant financial and regulatory risk.
